The Core Issue

The knowledge is in the technician's head. The problem is getting it out.

Technicians are automotive experts, not professional writers. Many operate with English as a Second Language (ESL). This mismatch creates massive operational bottlenecks.

Follow-up Bottlenecks

43% of repair orders require extra follow-up just to clarify what the technician wrote.

Advisor Burnout

Service Advisors waste an average of 17 hours per month deciphering and rewriting technical workshop notes.

Invoicing Delays

26% of invoices are typed while customers wait on-site, and 29% of non-waiting customers experience friction due to incomplete documentation.

Warranty Rework

45% of repair orders have to be re-processed at least once for warranty claims or customer copies.

Admin Burden

Admin tasks consume 7% of a technician's daily time—keeping them away from the tools.

Inconsistency

No standardized format exists across the workshop; every technician writes differently, leaving no support for ESL barriers.

Workflow

From Raw Input to Professional Story in 4 Steps

1

Choose Repair

Technician searches thousands of pre-built templates by keyword (e.g., "engine", "brakes") or launches the custom job form. Customer name, contact, and vehicle details pre-load instantly from the repair order.

2

AI Formats and Corrects

The GPT-4O Mini engine reads raw notes (even a simple sentence like "replaced the whole windscreen") and automatically corrects grammar, spelling, and punctuation.

3

Dual Output Generated

Simultaneously creates two versions side-by-side: a technical version structured in 3C format for warranty/RO, and a plain-language, jargon-free version for the customer invoice.

4

Advisor Reviews & Sends

Places a Human-in-the-Loop "Human Review Gate". All AI outputs are flagged with a note for the Service Advisor to review/edit before anything reaches the customer. Zero auto-sends.
